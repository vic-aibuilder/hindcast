# Hindcast — Seed Corpus Candidates

**Source:** Alex Mustonen calibration document (Jun 1, 2026)
**Sub-slice:** Sneaker/streetwear + DTC lifestyle (converging register)

**Status:** Confirmed saturated examples — primary candidates for seed corpus extraction

These spaces were identified by Alex as representative of the DTC/athletic lifestyle convergence pattern. They are strong candidates for the seed corpus because they are well-documented, widely photographed, and exhibit the saturation moves Hindcast is designed to surface.

## Why These Spaces

Alex's framing: "Walk through SoHo and you'll notice it: a cluster of lifestyle brands, all ostensibly distinct, all sharing the same spatial language. Bleached white walls, blonde wood fixtures, terrazzo or concrete floors, minimal product display, maybe a plant. This is Filterworld made physical — algorithmic convergence expressed in square footage."

The convergence pattern across these spaces:
- **Material:** blonde wood / light oak, bleached white / bright white walls, terrazzo or poured concrete floors
- **Fixtures:** chrome rail or matte black rail — stock fixture systems with no custom thought
- **Form:** sparse product display, architectural plant as finishing touch
- **Atmosphere:** neutral / clean-slate palette, warm lighting, residential-adjacent formality

---

## Retrieval Priority

These spaces should be prioritized in the seed corpus extraction pass because:
1. They are the clearest examples of the saturation pattern in the sneaker/DTC lifestyle register
2. They are densely photographed in the confirmed publication list (Dezeen, Wallpaper, Architectural Digest)
3. They provide the contrast baseline against which less-saturated spaces read as distinct

When the seed corpus is built, aim for 2–4 images per space from different angles (entry, fixture system, floor/wall detail, full-room shot) to give the schema extractor enough signal per dimension.
