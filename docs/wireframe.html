01 — User Flow

How the tool moves from brief to output.

Four steps. The designer inputs a brief, runs the search, gets back a visual map of saturation and a short written brief, then iterates.

01
Input

A visual grid of what's saturated, plus a short diagnostic brief. The tool maps the landscape. The designer decides what to do with it.

01 / 03
The Plinth and the Wall
NYC sneaker retail consolidates around two opposed moves: the polished concrete or stone plinth holding a single pair under spot lighting, and the floor-to-ceiling salon wall, sole-out, dozens of pairs at once. Both treat the sneaker as something to be seen — one as art object, the other as archive.
Of the two, the salon wall is the dominant register; single-pair gallery plinths appear in fewer than 20% of the set, typically reserved for hero drops or one-of-one collaborations. Mid-density mixed merchandising — a handful of pairs on varied display — is essentially absent.

02 / 03
The Raw Industrial Shell
Exposed concrete, raw steel scaffold fixtures, bare ceilings with painted ductwork. The space reads unfinished by design — a found warehouse dressed minimally, product as the only warmth in the room.
Of the three dominant moves, the raw-industrial shell is the least populated in the corpus. It surfaces primarily in skate-and-workwear-adjacent brands — Supreme, Carhartt, Union — where the unfinished aesthetic reads as continuous with the brand's outsider posture. Among streetwear brands chasing luxury legibility, the move is essentially absent.

03 / 03
The Heritage Maximalist
Wood paneling, brass detailing, stone floors, decorative lighting — sneaker retail dressed as private club or heritage house. The sneaker sits among curated artifacts (vintage furniture, hardcover books, sometimes a car) rather than alone on a plinth. The brand codes itself as institution, not store.
Stripped-back or raw-shell interiors appear in fewer than 15% of the set among NYC's most-photographed sneaker retail. The dominant visual register is fully-resolved, not deliberately undone.
